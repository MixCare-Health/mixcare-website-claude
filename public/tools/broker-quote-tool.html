Individual underwriting rules

  1. Medical declaration required at application. Health questionnaire (10 questions) covers chronic conditions, recent hospitalisations, pregnancy, mental health.
  2. 90-day waiting period from policy effective date for non-emergency outpatient care. Accident-related visits covered after 30 days.
  3. 12-month pre-existing condition exclusion. Conditions disclosed at application or detected within first year are excluded from cover.
  4. Age range: 18 minimum, 64 maximum at first underwriting. Renewable to age 75 with reunderwriting at each renewal.
  5. Annual reunderwriting: premium reviewed each year based on age band shift and individual claims experience. Up to +20% loading applied if claims exceed expected.
  6. Single-life policy: covers the named applicant only. Family options (spouse / children rider) available as a separate product (not in this tool).
  7. Cooling-off period: 21 days from policy issue, full refund if no claim made.
  8. Premium payment: annual in advance, or quarterly with 2% surcharge.
  9. Excluded conditions: as group plan + suicide / self-inflicted injury, professional sports, war/terrorism, cosmetic procedures, fertility treatment.

Underwriting rules

  1. Mandatory enrolment of the whole group at the quoted price. Voluntary participation triggers +15% adverse-selection loading. Underwriting waiver only available at 100% participation.
  2. 12-month plan term, renewable annually. Renewal pricing rebased using 80% actual-claims credibility + 20% market benchmark.
  3. Stop-loss at HK$5,000 per claim per employee — protects MixCare and the client from catastrophic single events.
  4. Loss-ratio threshold: if claims ÷ premium exceeds 85% on any tier, MixCare triggers a copay or visit-cap adjustment conversation at the 6-month review.
  5. Minimum group size: 5 employees. Groups under 30 EEs receive a 5% small-group loading.
  6. Age trigger: groups with average age above 50 are recommended to start at Plan C minimum.
  7. Referrals required: Specialist visits always require a GP referral on file. Plan B and C Physiotherapy require referral. Plan C X-ray/Lab and Extra Medication require referral.
  8. Network: Fullerton panel only — no off-panel reimbursement. Members pay full cost out-of-pocket if they go off-panel.
  9. Plan effective date: minimum 14 days from signed Service Agreement to allow onboarding (member ID issuance, app provisioning, employer briefing).
  10. Excluded from all tiers: hospitalisation, dental, optical, vaccinations, health screening, maternity, mental health (available as add-ons by separate agreement).

Risk register (top items)

  • Adverse selection: mitigated by mandatory enrolment.
  • Plan C utilisation creep at $30 GP copay: 30-visit cap absorbs the upper tail.
  • Specialist referral gaming: 6-month referral-pattern audit catches abuse.
  • Year-1 loss-making on Plan B/C: stop-loss at $5K + renewal repricing.
  • Caritas-specific older demographic: confirm avg age via census; apply age factor automatically.
  • Network leakage: no off-panel benefit — confirm Fullerton density in NT North + Kowloon East before signing.

Year-1 monitoring

  • Monthly: visit count per EE per service, copay collection rate, panel utilisation by clinic, referral compliance.
  • Quarterly: loss ratio by tier, specialist referral patterns, X-ray utilisation (Plan C), member app engagement.
  • 6-month review: trigger copay/cap adjustment if loss ratio > 85%; rebate or upgrade conversation if utilisation < 70% of expectation.
  • Renewal: 80% claims-experience credibility + 20% market benchmark; demographic shift adjustment if avg age changed by ≥ 2 years.
